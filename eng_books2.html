What does “face” mean to the Chinese? What are its deep implications in communication with the Chinese and business?

This concise and practical guide provides an in-depth understanding of the cultural importance of "saving face" or “not losing face” in China.

It covers the most frequent situations of misunderstandings between Chinese and Westerners. It is not simply a “do or don’t” guide, nor a scientific paper on the concept of face. It is full of real life anecdotes and cultural faux-pas shared by both Chinese and Westerners (Europeans and Americans) collected and analyzed over several years of interviewsand research.

Why write a book about “face” in China?

During my 8 years in China, I realized that there were many problems of communication between Chinese and Westerners that came directly from “face” issues. Although everyone working or living in China knows “face” is an essential part in the Chinese culture and in every social interaction, this concept is often misinterpreted or not well understood. So I decided to fill the gap, in order to help people working or living in China, who are confronted to face issues nearly every day.
